Research Theme

The Challenge: Overcrowded Classrooms

Recent advances in LLMs offer a solution to the "Teacher's Dilemma": How to provide individual attention to 40+ students simultaneously?

!

Current Problems

  • • Reduced student engagement due to lack of attention.
  • • Heterogeneous learning outcomes (gap widens).
  • • Excessive repetitive workload for teachers.
  • • Difficulty in providing timely feedback.
✓

Proposed Solution

  • • Personalized Assistant: Adapts to student level.
  • • Scaffolding: Guided support, not just answers.
  • • Teacher Support: Reduces repetitive questions.
  • • Complementary: Assists, does not replace.

Defining Roles: Symbiosis, Not Replacement

Dimension The Human Teacher The AI Assistant
Primary Focus Pedagogical strategy, emotional support, complex concepts. Repetitive explanations, immediate feedback, practice drills.
Scalability Limited (1-to-Many). Infinite (1-to-1 for every student).
Adaptability Adapts to the "average" of the class. Adapts to individual profile & history.

Execution Plan

Methodology & Roadmap

An applied research approach combining system design, data engineering, and experimental evaluation.

Phase 1

Needs Analysis & System Design

Before writing code, we must understand the classroom dynamics. This phase involves defining the functional architecture and key use cases.

Actions
  • • Teacher/Student interviews & questionnaires.
  • • Identify friction points in large classes.
  • • Design functional architecture (Dashboard, Chat UI).
Deliverables
  • • Requirement Specification Document.
  • • UI/UX Wireframes.
  • • Architectural Diagram.
Phase 2

Data Collection & Preparation

High-quality pedagogical data is essential for fine-tuning. We focus on materials that encourage "step-by-step" thinking.

Data Sources

Curricula, textbooks, exercises, model solutions, and anonymized teacher-student interaction logs (where authorized).

Processing

Cleaning, Anonymization, Formatting for Instruction Tuning (Input/Output pairs).

Phase 3

Model Adaptation & Integration

Tuning the generic LLM to become a "Pedagogical Agent" that provides hints rather than direct answers.

  • 1
    Model Selection

    Selecting a base model (e.g., Llama, Mistral) based on size and licensing.

  • 2
    Fine-Tuning

    Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) to encourage structured explanations and Socratic questioning.

  • 3
    Safety Guardrails

    Rule-based constraints to prevent hallucination and ensure appropriate content.

Phase 4

Personalization Mechanisms

The core differentiator: The system remembers the student.

Student Profile

Stores academic level, past interactions, and recurring error patterns.

Dynamic Adjustment

Adjusts vocabulary complexity, explanation depth, and example relevance in real-time.

Phase 5

Experimental Evaluation

Testing the prototype in a real educational setting (Pilot Study).

Evaluation Methods
Quantitative
Pre/Post Tests, Usage Stats
Qualitative
Interviews, Surveys
Control Group
Comparison with non-assisted class

Expected Outcomes

We aim to validate a prototype that improves learning without burdening teachers. The chart below illustrates the target metrics for success.

Evaluation Criteria

For Students

Understanding, Engagement, Confidence to ask questions.

For Teachers

Perceived workload, Trust in AI, Usefulness.

⚖️ Ethical Considerations

Special attention will be given to data privacy and the role of AI.

  • Anonymization of all student data.
  • Informed consent from all participants.
  • Mechanisms to report incorrect AI responses.
  • Principle: AI supports, never replaces.
